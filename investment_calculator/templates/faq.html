Frequently Asked Questions


What is compounding?

Compounding refers to the process where the value of an investment increases over time due to earnings on both the initial principal and the accumulated interest from previous periods. Essentially, you earn "interest on interest."

How does this calculator work?

This calculator estimates the future value of your investments based on the initial amount, monthly contributions, investment duration, annual interest rate, and inflation rate.

What is the future value?

The future value is the estimated amount you will have at the end of the investment period, taking into account the initial investment and the effect of compounding over time.

What is total contribution?

Total contribution refers to the total amount of money you have invested over time, including your initial investment and any additional contributions made throughout the investment period.

What is inflation?

Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services rises, eroding purchasing power. It affects the real return on your investments.

How does inflation affect investments?

Inflation can significantly impact the real return on investments. If your investment returns are lower than the inflation rate, the purchasing power of your money decreases. It's essential to consider inflation when calculating future values.

What is the formula used for calculations?

The calculations follow the formulas below:

$$P(1+r)^t \hspace{0.5cm} (1)$$

The monthly contributions \( C \) (times 12) are compounded from \( t - 1 \) to \( 0 \) years:

$$12C \cdot (1+r)^{t-1} + 12C \cdot (1+r)^{t-2} + \cdots + 12C \cdot (1+r)^0$$

The sum of that series is:

$$12C \cdot \dfrac{(1+r)^t-1}{(1+r)-1}$$

Then simplify:

$$\dfrac{12C}{r} \left[(1+r)^t-1\right] \hspace{0.5cm} (2)$$

Put together \( (1) \) and \( (2) \):

$$\underbrace{P(1+r)^t}_{\text{(1)}} \ \ + \ \ \underbrace{\dfrac{12C}{r} \left[ (1+r)^t - 1 \right]}_{\text{(2)}}$$

We get the Future Value:

$$\text{FV} = P(1+r)^t + \dfrac{12C}{r} \left[ (1+r)^t - 1 \right]$$

To adjust for inflation, the final formula is modified to:

$$\text{FV$_{\text{adjusted}}$} = \dfrac{\text{FV}}{(1+i)^t} $$

Variables Explained

  • $$ P = \text{Initial investment (in USD)} $$
  • $$ r = \text{Annual interest rate (as a decimal)} $$
  • $$ t = \text{Length of time (in years)} $$
  • $$ C = \text{Monthly contributions (in USD)} $$
  • $$ i = \text{Inflation rate (as a decimal)} $$

Is this calculator accurate?

This calculator provides estimates based on user inputs and does not account for market fluctuations or other factors affecting real investment returns.
